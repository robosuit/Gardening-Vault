# 🌼 Companion Plant Guilds

Plant guilds are groups of plants that thrive together, supporting each other's growth and creating ecological synergy.

---

## 🐝 Pollinator Guild

Plants that attract beneficial insects and pollinators.

**Plants:**
- [[Borage]]
- [[Bergamot]]
- [[Echinacea]]
- [[Lavender]]
- [[White Yarrow]]

**Why Together:**
- Attract bees, butterflies, hoverflies
- Extended bloom periods (Feb–Oct)
- Create visual harmony with mixed colors
- Support integrated pest management

**Planting:** Mix throughout garden beds. Space according to mature size.

**Care:** Deadhead spent flowers to extend blooms. Let some flowers go to seed for fall visitors.

---

## 🌿 Mediterranean Dry Guild

Heat-loving, drought-tolerant herbs that share similar growing conditions.

**Plants:**
- [[Thyme]]
- [[Oregano]]
- [[Rosemary]]
- [[Sage]]
- [[Marjoram]]

**Why Together:**
- All prefer full sun (6+ hours)
- All drought-tolerant (low-water zone)
- Similar fertilizer needs (low)
- Mediterranean flavor profile (culinary overlap)

**Planting:** Group in single bed or corner. Ensure excellent drainage (add sand if needed).

**Care:** Prune regularly to prevent woody growth. Minimal water once established.

**Propagation:** All root easily from cuttings.

---

## 🍽️ Digestive Guild

Culinary herbs that support digestive function, often used together in teas and preparations.

**Plants:**
- [[Anise]]
- [[Fennel]]
- [[Caraway]]
- [[Dill]]
- [[Cilantro]]

**Why Together:**
- All support digestive health
- Aromatic/volatile oil characteristics
- Often combined in traditional digestive teas
- Similar planting seasons (spring/cool weather)

**Planting:** Plant in succession (Feb, March, April) for continuous harvest.

**Care:** Expect self-seeding. Mark plants to harvest before full seed production, or save seed intentionally.

**Harvest:** Use fresh or dried. Peak potency when flowering.

## 🌱 Mint Containment Guild

High-vigor mints that need dedicated space to prevent garden takeover.

**Plants:**
- [[Peppermint]]
- [[Lemon Mint]]
- [[Lemon Balm]]
- [[Catnip]]
- [[Mountain Mint]]

**Why Together:**
- All aggressively spread via rhizomes
- All prefer similar moisture levels (medium)
- All thrive in partial shade
- Can be contained in same bed with barriers

**Planting:** Use raised beds or containers with barriers. Plant minimum 3 feet apart, or use contained pots.

**Care:** Regular harvesting controls spread. Cut back hard in fall.

**Propagation:** Divide in spring or fall. Root cuttings easily in water.

**Warning:** Do NOT plant mint directly in ground without serious containment strategy.

## 🌻 Shade-Tolerant Guild

Herbs that accept partial shade and are useful for less-sunny garden areas.

**Plants:**
- [[Lemon Balm]]
- [[Mint family]]
- [[Catnip]]
- [[Bergamot]]

**Growing:** Partial shade (2-4 hours sun). More shade = slower growth but often richer flavor.

## 💪 Immune Support Guild

Herbs traditionally used together for seasonal immune support.

**Plants:**
- [[Echinacea]]
- [[Thyme]]
- [[Oregano]]
- [[Sage]]
- [[Hyssop]]

**Why Together:**
- All containantimicrobial properties
- Support respiratory + immune systems
- Often combined in immune-support blends
- Vary in flavor (bitter, spicy, soothing)

**Preparation:** Create as tea blend, tincture, or syrup for winter support.

**Designing Your Garden:**

You don't need to plant entire guilds. Mix guilds strategically:

- **Sunny, dry corner:** Mediterranean guild
- **Shaded, moist area:** Mint containment (in containers)
- **Center focal point:** Pollinator guild for visual impact
- **Herb spiral:** Digestive guild at base, Mediterranean at top
- **Medicinal focus:** Immune support guild

Create your own combinations based on space and goals.
