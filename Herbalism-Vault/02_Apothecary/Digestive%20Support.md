## 📚 Traditional Digestive Tea Combinations

### Classic "Three Seed" (European Traditional)
- 1 part [[Anise]] seed
- 1 part [[Fennel]] seed
- 1 part [[Caraway]] seed

**Preparation:** 1 tbsp blend per 8 oz water, steep 10 minutes  
**Use:** After meals for digestion  
**Taste:** Warm, slightly sweet, licorice notes  

### Cooling Digestive (for heat/inflammation)
- 1 part [[Peppermint]]
- 1 part [[Cilantro]]
- ½ part [[Chamomile]]

**Use:** For inflammatory GI conditions  
**Taste:** Fresh, cooling, floral  

### Warming Digestive (for sluggish digestion)
- 1 part [[Fennel]]
- 1 part [[Ginger]] (kitchen)
- ½ part [[Marjoram]]

**Use:** For slow digestion, weak appetite  
**Taste:** Warm, spicy, herbaceous

## 💊 Primary Medicinal Actions

### Carminative
Reduces gas production and promotes its expulsion.

**Plants:** Anise, Fennel, Caraway, Dill, Summer Savory  
**How:** Volatile oils stimulate GI secretions  
**Use:** After meals or when bloating occurs  

### Antispasmodic
Relaxes digestive tract muscle tension and cramping.

**Plants:** Peppermint, Chamomile, Anise, Fennel, Dill  
**How:** Relaxes smooth muscle of GI tract  
**Use:** For cramps, spasms, IBS-related discomfort  

### Bitter Tonic
Stimulates digestive secretions and appetite.

**Plants:** Marjoram, Summer Savory (slightly)  
**How:** Bitter taste triggers digestive enzyme production  
**Use:** Before meals to prepare digestion  

### Anti-inflammatory
Reduces inflammation of GI lining.

**Plants:** Chamomile, Peppermint  
**How:** Flavonoid and polysaccharide content  
**Use:** For chronic GI inflammation

## 🧪 Preparation Methods for Digestion

### Daily Digestive Tea
**Ratio:** 1–2 tbsp herb per 8 oz water  
**Steep:** 10–15 minutes  
**Frequency:** 1–3 times daily, after meals  
**Storage:** Fresh daily  

### Tincture (Concentrated Extract)
**Ratio:** 1:5 dried herbs in 40% alcohol  
**Dose:** 10–20 drops before meals  
**Benefit:** More concentrated, stronger action  
**Storage:** 3+ years in cool, dark place  

### Glycerin Extract (Alcohol-Free)
**Ratio:** Same as tincture but use vegetable glycerin  
**Dose:** 1 tsp before meals  
**Benefit:** Gentle, no alcohol  
**Storage:** 2–3 years  

### Infused Oil (External)
**Method:** Infuse [[Chamomile]] in olive oil for GI massage  
**Application:** Gentle abdominal massage  
**Benefit:** Relaxing + topical absorption

## 🌡️ Energetic Considerations

**For:** Cold digestion, sluggish appetite, slow movement

**Preparation:** Hot tea  
**Frequency:** Regular use

### Cooling Digestive Herbs
**For:** Hot digestion, inflammation, acid reflux  
**Herbs:** Peppermint, Cilantro, Chamomile  
**Preparation:** Room temperature or cool tea  
**Frequency:** As needed

## ⚠️ Safety Notes

**Generally Safe:** All herbs listed here are well-tolerated  
**Pregnancy:** Culinary amounts safe; large medicinal doses consult provider  
**Breastfeeding:** Safe in culinary use  
**Drug Interactions:** Generally none; peppermint may interact with iron absorption  
**Overdose:** Unlikely but excess may cause burning sensation in mouth  

**Contraindications:**
- GERD or ulcers: Use cooling herbs, avoid warming
- Mint allergy: Avoid peppermint
- Bile duct obstruction: Consult provider
- Weak digestion: Start with small amounts

## 🍽️ Culinary + Medicinal Use

Most digestive herbs serve double duty:

**Dill:** Seeds in pickles, leaves in fish dishes  
**Cilantro:** Fresh in salads, salsas  
**Anise/Fennel Seeds:** In breads, teas, as after-dinner chew  
**Peppermint:** Tea, desserts, fresh in water  
**Basil variants:** Cooking + medicinal  

*Using herbs regularly in cooking means ongoing digestive support without formal "medicine" approach.*
