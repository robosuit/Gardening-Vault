# 🧠 Nervine & Calming Herbs

Herbs that support the nervous system, reduce anxiety, promote relaxation, and support restorative sleep.

---

## 🌿 Nervine Herbs

### [[Lavender]]
- Gentle calming action
- Supports sleep
- Cooling, slightly dry
- Works internally + topically

### [[Lemon Balm]]
- Uplifting nervine
- Mild antispasmodic
- Cooling, moist
- Taste: Pleasant, lemony

### [[Catnip]]
- Gentle sedative
- Reduces anxiety + tension
- Cooling
- Safe for children

### [[Chamomile]]
- Soothing, nervine + digestive
- Supports sleep
- Anti-inflammatory
- Gentle on all ages

### [[Marjoram]]
- Warming nervine
- Eases tension
- Slightly bitter
- Traditional European use

## 💊 Primary Medicinal Actions

### Nervine (Nervous System Support)
Supports overall nervous system function and resilience.

**Plants:** All listed above  
**How:** Reduce hyperactivity, increase parasympathetic tone  
**Use:** Regular tea during stressful periods  

### Anxiolytic (Anxiety Reduction)
Reduces anxiety and nervous tension.

**Plants:** Lavender, Lemon Balm, Catnip  
**How:** GABA-like activity, sedative alkaloids  
**Use:** Daily or as needed during stress spikes  

### Hypnotic (Sleep Support)
Promotes restful sleep and reduces wakefulness.

**Plants:** Lavender, Catnip, Marjoram  
**How:** Mild sedative action  
**Use:** Evening only, 30 minutes before bed  

### Anti-inflammatory
Reduces inflammation in nervous tissue and beyond.

**Plants:** Lavender, Chamomile  
**How:** Flavonoid content  
**Use:** Chronic stress + inflammation support

## 🧪 Preparation Methods for Nervous System

### Daily Calming Tea
**Blend:** Equal parts [[Lemon Balm]], [[Chamomile]], [[Lavender]]  
**Ratio:** 1 tbsp blend per 8 oz water  
**Steep:** 10–15 minutes  
**Frequency:** Afternoon or evening, or 2–3x daily during stress  
**Temperature:** Warm (heat supports calming action)  
**Storage:** Fresh daily  

### Sleep-Support Tea
**Blend:** 
- 2 parts [[Lavender]]
- 2 parts [[Catnip]]
- 1 part [[Marjoram]]

**Preparation:** 1 tbsp blend per 8 oz hot water, steep 15 minutes  
**Dose:** 1 cup, 30–60 minutes before bed  
**Frequency:** Nightly as needed  

### Tincture (Concentrated)
**Ratio:** 1:5 dried herbs in 40% alcohol  
**Dose:** 20–30 drops, 2–3x daily  
**Benefit:** Stronger, faster-acting  
**Use:** During acute stress or anxiety  
**Storage:** 3–5 years in cool, dark place  

### Herbal Glycerin (Alcohol-Free)
**Ratio:** Same as tincture but vegetable glycerin  
**Dose:** 1 tsp, 2–3x daily  
**Benefit:** Gentle, sweet-tasting, supports children  
**Storage:** 2–3 years  

### Infused Oil for Massage
**Method:** Infuse [[Lavender]] in olive oil, 4 weeks  
**Use:** Gentle chest/shoulder/temple massage  
**Benefit:** Topical + aromatherapeutic  
**Best:** Evening wind-down ritual  

### Herbal Bath
**Method:** Steep large infusion of calming blend, strain into bath water  
**Time:** 15–20 minutes  
**Benefit:** Full-body relaxation, skin absorption  
**Best:** Evening before bed  

### Honey-Herb Elixir
**Method:** Make strong infusion, strain, mix 1:1 with raw honey  
**Dose:** 1 tsp, 2–3x daily  
**Benefit:** Palatable, sustained energy  
**Storage:** 6+ months

## 🌡️ Energetic Profiles

### Cooling Nervines
**For:** Overheated, inflamed, irritable  
**Herbs:** Lavender, Lemon Balm, Chamomile, Catnip  
**Best:** Summer, hot climates, hotheaded people  
**Preparation:** Room temperature or cool tea  

### Warming Nervines
**For:** Cold, depleted, sluggish mood  
**Herbs:** Marjoram  
**Best:** Winter, cold climates, sluggish temperament  
**Preparation:** Hot tea, taken warm

## 🛌 Sleep-Support Protocol

### 2–3 Hours Before Bed
- Light dinner (avoid heavy foods)
- Sip warm calming tea

### 30–60 Minutes Before Bed
- Sleep-support tea or tincture
- Relaxing activity (reading, gentle stretching)
- Dim lights, cool room (60–67°F ideal)

### Topical Support
- Lavender oil on pillow
- Calming herbal massage oil on temples

### In Bed
- Body scan or gentle breathing (4-count in, 6-count out)
- Expect sleep within 20–40 minutes

---

## 😰 Anxiety-Support Protocol

### Acute Anxiety
- Take nervine tincture (fast-acting)
- Breathe slowly (4-count in, 4-count hold, 6-count out)
- Move body gently (walk, stretch)
- Sip warm calming tea if safe

### Chronic Stress/Anxiety
- Daily calming tea, 2–3x
- Consistent sleep schedule
- Regular exercise
- Weekly herbal bath
- Monthly reassessment

## ⚠️ Safety & Contraindications

**Generally Safe:**
- All herbs listed here are well-tolerated
- Safe for children (appropriate doses)
- Safe during pregnancy/breastfeeding (consult provider for tinctures)

**Cautions:**
- **Sedation:** Don't drive after strong doses
- **Pregnancy:** Use culinary amounts; large medicinal doses consult OB
- **Medications:** Some herbs may interact with SSRIs, sedatives (consult provider)
- **Allergies:** Ragweed-allergic individuals may react to chamomile
- **Over-reliance:** Use herbs to support, not replace, professional mental health care

**When to Seek Professional Help:**
- Persistent anxiety lasting >2 weeks
- Thoughts of self-harm
- Severe sleep disruption
- Depression symptoms
- Consider cognitive behavioral therapy + herbs as integrated approach
