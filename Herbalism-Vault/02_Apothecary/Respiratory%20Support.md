# 🫁 Respiratory Support Herbs

Herbs that support lung function, ease cough, promote mucus clearance, and strengthen respiratory resilience.

- Antimicrobial
- Warming
- Culinary + medicinal

### [[Oregano]]
- Antimicrobial + expectorant
- Warming action
- Volatile oils support lung clearing
- Culinary herb

### [[Sage]]

- Drying (reduces phlegm)

- Supports respiratory health

### [[Anise]]
- Expectorant

- Sweet taste (helps children)

## 💊 Primary Medicinal Actions

### Expectorant
Helps loosen mucus so it can be coughed up more easily.

**Plants:** Hyssop, Horehound, Thyme, Oregano, Anise  
**How:** Volatile oils irritate mucous membranes, triggering productive cough  
**Use:** During congestion, cough with thick sticky mucus  

### Antitussive (Cough Suppressant)
Reduces unproductive/dry coughing that disrupts sleep.

**Plants:** Horehound, Thyme (anise for children)  
**How:** Relaxes cough reflex  
**Use:** Dry, hacking cough, nighttime cough  

### Antimicrobial
Fights bacterial + viral respiratory infections.

**Plants:** Oregano, Sage, Thyme, Echinacea  
**How:** Volatile oil + flavonoid content  
**Use:** Alongside immune support  

### Anti-inflammatory
Reduces inflamed respiratory tissues.

**Plants:** Echinacea, Thyme, Oregano  
**How:** Polysaccharides + flavonoids  
**Use:** Chronic respiratory conditions, seasonal challenges  

### Immune Modulating
Strengthens overall immune response.

**Plants:** Echinacea (primary)  
**How:** Enhances white blood cell activity  
**Use:** Prevention + acute support  

### Astringent
Dries excess secretions (phlegm, postnasal drip).

**Plants:** Sage, Horehound  
**How:** Tannin content tightens tissues  
**Use:** Sore throat, excess mucus

## 🧪 Preparation Methods for Respiratory Health

### Respiratory Support Tea
**Blend:**
- 2 parts [[Thyme]]
- 1 part [[Horehound]]
- 1 part [[Hyssop]]
- ½ part [[Ginger]] (from kitchen)

**Preparation:** 1 tbsp blend per 8 oz water, steep 10–15 minutes  
**Dose:** 1–3 cups daily during respiratory challenge  
**Taste:** Warm, slightly bitter, spicy  
**Best:** Hot (heat supports opening airways)  

### Sore Throat Gargle
**Brew:** 2 tbsp [[Sage]] + 1 tbsp [[Thyme]] per 8 oz water, simmer 5 minutes  
**Strain:** Cool to warm temperature  
**Use:** Gargle 30 seconds, 3–4x daily  
**Duration:** Until symptoms resolve  

### Cough Syrup (Traditional)
**Method:**
1. Make strong decoction: 2 tbsp herbs per cup water, simmer 20 minutes
2. Strain thoroughly
3. Add 1 cup raw honey (equal ratio or more)
4. Mix well

**Dose:** 1 tsp every 2–3 hours as needed  
**Storage:** Refrigerated, 2–3 months  
**Best:** For dry/hacking cough, especially at night

### Tincture (Concentrated)
**Ratio:** 1:5 dried respiratory herbs in 40% alcohol  
**Dose:** 20–30 drops, 3–4x daily  
**Benefit:** Stronger, alcohol preserves volatile oils  
**Storage:** 3–5 years  
**Best:** During active respiratory infection  

### Herbal Inhalation (Steam)
**Method:**
1. Steep 1–2 tbsp dried herbs in 2 cups hot water
2. Create steam tent with towel
3. Inhale steam 5–10 minutes
4. Breathe deeply

**Herbs:** [[Thyme]], [[Oregano]], [[Hyssop]]  
**Benefit:** Direct to lungs, anti-inflammatory + antimicrobial  
**Best:** Morning + evening during congestion  

### Honey-Herb Elixir
**Method:**
1. Make strong infusion, cool
2. Mix 1:1 with raw honey
3. Add 1 tbsp apple cider vinegar per cup (optional, enhances)

**Dose:** 1 tsp, 3–4x daily or as needed  
**Storage:** 6+ months  
**Best:** Palatable, especially for children  

### Herbal Chest Rub Oil
**Method:** Infuse dried respiratory herbs in olive oil, 4 weeks  
**Application:** Warm oil, massage over chest + upper back  
**Benefit:** Topical absorption + warmth  
**Best:** Bedtime for respiratory ease

## 🛡️ Immune-Respiratory Protocol

### Prevention (During Cold/Flu Season)
- Daily [[Echinacea]] tea or tincture, 3x weekly
- Thyme + Oregano in cooking
- Adequate sleep, movement, stress management
- Handwashing, hygiene basics

### Early Symptoms (Caught Within 24–48 Hours)
- Start [[Echinacea]] tincture 30 drops, 4x daily
- Respiratory tea 3x daily
- Rest, fluids, light movement
- Expectation: Duration cut by 30–50%

### Active Infection
- Respiratory tea 3–4x daily
- Echinacea tincture 30 drops, 4–6x daily
- Cough syrup as needed
- Herbal steam twice daily
- Rest, nutrition support
- Consider professional care if worsening

### Post-Infection Recovery
- Continue respiratory tea 1–2x daily
- Echinacea for 2 additional weeks
- Nutritive herbs ([[Nettle]], other mineral-rich)
- Gentle movement, gradual return to normal activity

## ⚠️ Safety & Contraindications

**Generally Safe:**
- All herbs listed are well-tolerated
- Safe for children (appropriate doses)
- Supportive alongside conventional care

**Cautions:**
- **Horehound:** Bitter; not for those with bitter sensitivity
- **Echinacea:** Some sources suggest avoiding >4 weeks (though research mixed)
- **Allergies:** Some individuals allergic to Echinacea (Asteraceae family)
- **Thyroid conditions:** Echinacea may affect; consult provider
- **Autoimmune:** Echinacea immune-modulating; consult before use

**When to Seek Conventional Care:**
- Fever >103°F
- Cough producing blood
- Severe dyspnea (shortness of breath)
- Symptoms worsening after 7 days
- Immunocompromised individuals
- Young infants or elderly high-risk individuals

**Integration:** Herbs complement conventional care; not replacements for antibiotics when needed.
