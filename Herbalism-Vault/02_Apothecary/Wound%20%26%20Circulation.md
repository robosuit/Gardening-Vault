# 🩹 Wound & Circulation Herbs

Herbs that support wound healing, promote tissue repair, and enhance circulatory health.

---

## 🌿 Wound & Circulation Herbs

### [[Lavender]]
- Tissue healing (skin repair)
- Antimicrobial + anti-inflammatory
- Cooling, slightly dry
- Topical primary use

### [[White Yarrow]]
- Wound healing + astringent
- Circulation stimulating
- Antifungal
- Drying action

### [[Echinacea]]
- Immune support healing process
- Anti-inflammatory
- Lymphatic stimulating
- Works systemically

### [[Sage]]
- Astringent (tightens tissue)
- Antimicrobial
- Wound specific
- Sore throat topical

## 💊 Primary Medicinal Actions

### Vulnerary (Wound Healing)
Promotes tissue repair and skin regeneration.

**Plants:** Lavender, White Yarrow, Echinacea  
**How:** Stimulate collagen production, increase blood flow to wound  
**Use:** Cuts, scrapes, slow-healing wounds  

### Astringent
Tightens tissues, reduces bleeding, tightens skin elasticity.

**Plants:** White Yarrow, Sage  
**How:** Tannin content binds proteins, constricts tissue  
**Use:** Bleeding wounds, excessive secretions  

### Antimicrobial
Prevents infection in wounds.

**Plants:** Lavender, Sage, Echinacea, White Yarrow  
**How:** Volatile oils + polysaccharides inhibit pathogens  
**Use:** Prevention of infection, infected wounds  

### Anti-inflammatory
Reduces inflammation, redness, heat around wounding.

**Plants:** All listed  
**How:** Flavonoid + polysaccharide content  
**Use:** Hot, inflamed wounds or areas  

### Circulatory Stimulating
Enhances blood flow to tissues, promoting delivery of oxygen + nutrients.

**Plants:** White Yarrow, Echinacea (lymphatic)  
**How:** Promote vasodilation, lymphatic flow  
**Use:** Sluggish healing, poor circulation, leg ulcers  

### Lymphatic Stimulating
Moves lymphatic fluid, supports immune clearance.

**Plants:** Echinacea (primary)  
**How:** Polysaccharides stimulate lymph movement  
**Use:** Swollen lymph nodes, post-infection recovery

## 🧪 Preparation Methods for Wound Healing

### Fresh Wound Immediate Care
**Method:**
1. Rinse under clean water
2. Apply [[Lavender]] essential oil directly (diluted in carrier oil if large area) or use lavender-infused oil
3. Cover if needed
4. Repeat 2–3x daily

**Best:** Minor cuts, small scrapes  
**Duration:** Until healed, typically 5–10 days  

### Herbal Salve (Best for Wounds)
**Recipe:**
1. Infuse 1 oz dried [[Lavender]] in 1 cup olive oil, 4 weeks in sun/warm place
2. Strain thoroughly
3. Melt 1 oz beeswax in salve mixture
4. Pour into container, cool until solid

**Application:** Apply directly to cleaned wound, 2–3x daily  
**Coverage:** Leave uncovered when possible (air improves healing) or use sterile gauze  
**Storage:** Cool place, 6+ months  
**Best:** Small wounds, minor cuts, abrasions  

### Herbal Infused Oil (Vulnerary Oil)
**Recipe:**
1. Infuse 1 oz dried Lavender + ½ oz dried White Yarrow in 1 cup olive oil
2. Sit in warm place 4–6 weeks
3. Strain, bottle, store

**Application:** Apply to cleaned wound or affected area  
**Best:** Daily wound care, massage for circulation  

### Poultice (Fresh Herbs, Immediate)
**Method:**
1. Crush fresh [[Plantain]] leaves (if available) OR [[Lavender]] or [[Yarrow]]
2. Apply directly to wound
3. Cover with sterile gauze
4. Hold 5–10 minutes

**Best:** Fresh wounds, splinters, stubborn wounds  
**Frequency:** Every 2–4 hours as needed  

### Herbal Tea (Internal Circulation Support)
**Blend (Internally):**
- 1 part [[Echinacea]]
- 1 part [[White Yarrow]]
- 1 part [[Ginger]] (circulation)

**Preparation:** 1 tbsp blend per 8 oz water, steep 10–15 minutes  
**Dose:** 1–3 cups daily during wound healing  
**Duration:** For 2–4 weeks during active healing  
**Best:** Supporting tissue repair from within  

### Tincture (Circulation Support)
**Ratio:** 1:5 dried White Yarrow or Echinacea in 40% alcohol  
**Dose:** 20–30 drops, 2–3x daily  
**Benefit:** Systemic support, long shelf life  
**Use:** Chronic wounds, poor circulation, general healing support  

### Herbal Wash (Infected or Slow-Healing)
**Method:**
1. Brew strong decoction: 2 tbsp dried Lavender + Yarrow, simmer 20 minutes
2. Strain, cool to warm
3. Clean wound with herbal wash, 2–3x daily

**Best:** Mildly infected, slow-healing wounds  
**Assessment:** If signs of serious infection, seek professional care

## 🩺 Circulation Support Protocol

### Poor Circulation Prevention
- Herbal tea blend 1x daily (Yarrow + Echinacea + Ginger)
- Regular movement (walking, yoga)
- Reduce stress
- Adequate water intake

### Slow-Healing Wound
1. Internal: Circulation tea 2–3x daily
2. Topical: Lavender salve 3x daily
3. External support: Elevate leg (if leg wound), compression if appropriate
4. Reassess in 10–14 days

### Post-Surgery Wound Care
- Clean with gentle herbal wash (Yarrow + Lavender)
- Apply salve 2–3x daily
- Internal circulation tea 2x daily
- Keep wound clean, follow surgical instructions
- Resume activity gradually as healed

### Minor Cuts/Scrapes
- Apply lavender salve immediately
- Reapply 2–3x daily
- Leave uncovered when possible
- Expected healing: 5–10 days

### Leg Ulcers / Chronic Wounds
- Professional assessment first (rule out serious underlying condition)
- Internal Echinacea tincture 3x daily
- Topical Lavender salve 3–4x daily
- Herbal wash 1–2x daily
- Consider systemic circulation support (diet, exercise, stress)
- Professional monitoring every 2–4 weeks

## ⚠️ Safety & Contraindications

**Generally Safe:**
- All herbs highly safe for topical use
- Internal use safe at appropriate doses

**Cautions:**
- **Echinacea:** Rare allergy to Asteraceae family (also includes ragweed)
- **Yarrow:** In rare cases may increase bleeding tendency; avoid if on blood thinners
- **Lavender:** Extremely rare photosensitivity; typically very safe
- **Pregnant/Nursing:** Topical use very safe; internal tinctures consult provider

**When to Seek Professional Care:**
- Wounds with signs of serious infection (increased heat, pus, red streaking)
- Large / deep wounds
- Wounds not progressing after 1–2 weeks
- Puncture wounds or animal bites
- Signs of tetanus or cellulitis
- Diabetic wounds (often need professional monitoring)
- Chronically slow-healing wounds (may indicate circulatory issues)

**Integration:** Herbs excellent for minor wounds and circulation support. Professional care still needed for serious injuries.
