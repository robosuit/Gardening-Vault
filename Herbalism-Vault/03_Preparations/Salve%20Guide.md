# 🧴 Salve Guide

A complete guide to making and using herbal salves, topical preparations with beeswax for sustained healing.

---

## What is a Salve?

A salve (also called balm or ointment) is a topical preparation combining infused oil with beeswax, creating a semi-solid consistency that adheres to skin and allows prolonged herbal contact.

**Advantages:**
- Long-lasting topical contact with affected area
- Moisture barrier (protects while and healing)
- Pleasant texture (often adds aromatics)
- Shelf-stable (months to years)
- Synergistic herb + beeswax benefits

**Best for:** Wounds, skin irritations, muscle soreness, topical healing

**Not ideal for:** Large-area treatment (cost), internal use

---

## Basic Salve Formula

### Ingredients

**Oil Base (choose one):**
- Olive oil (most common, mild)
- Coconut oil (mild, pleasant smell)
- Almond oil (light, absorbs well)
- Calendula-infused oil (adds healing action)

**Beeswax:**
- Raw beeswax (natural, unrefined)
- Beeswax pastilles (easier to measure)

**Herbal Extract:**
- Dried herbs (infused in oil)
- Essential oils (optional, for scent/extra action)

**Optional Additions:**
- Vitamin E oil (antioxidant, preservative)
- Honey (antimicrobial)
- Lanolin (if not vegan, adds plasticity)

## Salve Making: Step-by-Step

### Equipment Needed

**Essential:**
- Glass jars for oil infusion
- Double boiler or makeshift (glass bowl over pot of water)
- Thermometer (optional but helpful)
- Measuring cups/spoons
- Wooden spoon for stirring
- Glass or metal pour containers (warmed)
- Small tins or jars for finished salve
- Cheesecloth or fine strainer
- Labels

**Safety:**
- Apron (hot oil can splash)
- Heat-resistant gloves
- Paper towels for cleanup

---

### Method 1: Traditional Salve (Infused Oil + Beeswax)

**Time:** Oil infusion 4 weeks + 30 minutes salve-making

#### Step 1: Infuse Oil with Herbs (4 weeks)

**Choose herbs:**
- [[Lavender]] (soothing, antimicrobial)
- [[Calendula]] (wound healing, skin-supportive)
- [[White Yarrow]] (tissue healing)
- [[Plantain]] (if available, wound-healing)

**Infusion method:**

1. **Fill jar** with dried herb (loosely packed, 1/3–1/2 jar)
2. **Pour oil** until herb is covered by 2 inches
3. **Seal jar** with lid
4. **Infuse** in warm sunny location 4–6 weeks
   - Sun infusion (preferred): Full sun window
   - Warm-spot infusion: Near heater, warm cupboard
   - Cool infusion: Room temperature (longer, gentler)

5. **Check** weekly for moisture:
   - If condensation inside, prop open briefly to dry
   - Moisture + oil = rancidity risk

6. **Strain thoroughly** after 4 weeks:
   - Line strainer with cheesecloth
   - Pour through carefully
   - Press herb lightly (don't squeeze hard)
   - Discard herb matter
   - Filter through fine strainer if cloudy

7. **Store oil**:
   - Glass jar, cool place
   - Will last 6+ months
   - Use fresh oil for best results

#### Step 2: Make Salve (30 minutes)

**Ingredients:**
- 1 cup infused herbal oil
- 1 oz beeswax (approximately; adjust for consistency)

**Ratio Guide:**
- 4:1 oil to wax = soft, spreadable salve
- 3:1 oil to wax = medium-firm
- 2:1 oil to wax = firm, less greasy

*Start with 1 oz wax, can adjust after first batch*

**Process:**

1. **Set up double boiler:**
   - Fill bottom pot with water (not touching top pot when water boils)
   - Place glass bowl/top pot on bottom
   - Heat water to gentle simmer

2. **Measure ingredients:**
   - 1 cup infused oil
   - 1 oz beeswax (weighed or roughly by appearance)

3. **Add oil & beeswax to double boiler**
   - Pour oil into glass bowl
   - Add beeswax broken into small pieces
   - Stir occasionally until wax melts (10–15 minutes)
   - Don't exceed 170–180°F (preserves herbal compounds)

4. **Test consistency** (before adding to jars):
   - Drop small amount onto cool plate
   - Let sit 2 minutes
   - Check texture (should be soft but hold shape)
   - Too soft? Add more wax
   - Too hard? Add more oil
   - Return to heat, adjust, test again

5. **Prepare containers:**
   - Warm glass jars or tins (place near heat source)
   - Line up on clean surface
   - Prepare labels

6. **Pour salve:**
   - Once consistency is right, remove from heat
   - Carefully pour into warm containers (fills better, prevents air bubbles)
   - Don't fill to brim (air space prevents moisture)
   - Let cool completely before capping (30 minutes to 2 hours)

7. **Label & store:**
   - Label with herb(s), date made, suggested use
   - Store in cool place
   - Avoid direct sunlight

#### Shelf Life
6–12 months (or longer with vitamin E oil added)

### Method 2: Quick Salve (Pre-Made Oil)

**If you don't have time for 4-week infusion:**

**Use:**
- Buy pre-infused herbal oil (calendula oil available at natural food stores)
- OR use high-quality carrier oil with essential oil added

**Ingredients:**
- 1 cup pre-infused oil
- 1 oz beeswax

**Method:** Same as Method 1, Step 2 above

**Benefit:** Same-day creation possible

## Popular Herbal Salves

### Lavender Healing Salve

**Best for:** Minor wounds, skin irritation, soothing

**Making:**
1. Infuse 1 cup olive oil with 1 oz dried [[Lavender]] flowers, 4 weeks
2. Strain thoroughly
3. Make salve: 1 cup infused oil + 1 oz beeswax
4. Optional: Add 10 drops lavender essential oil for scent (after cooling slightly)

**Use:** Apply 2–3x daily to clean skin

**Shelf life:** 8–12 months

---

### Calendula Wound-Healing Salve

**Best for:** Wounds, skin repair, scarring

**Making:**
1. Infuse 1 cup olive oil with 1 oz dried [[Calendula]] petals, 4 weeks
2. Strain thoroughly (calendula petals can be sticky)
3. Make salve: 1 cup infused oil + 1 oz beeswax (may need slightly more wax)
4. Optional: Add 1 tbsp raw honey (extra antimicrobial) while warm

**Use:** Apply 2–3x daily to clean wounds or minor skin damage

**Shelf life:** 8–12 months

---

### Herbal Anti-Itch Salve

**Best for:** Dry skin, minor itching, eczema-prone skin

**Making:**
1. Infuse 1 cup coconut oil (more soothing) with:
   - ½ oz [[Lavender]]
   - ½ oz [[Plantain]] (if available)
   - ¼ oz [[Chamomile]]
   4 weeks

2. Make salve: 1 cup infused oil + 1 oz beeswax

3. Optional: Add 5 drops chamomile essential oil + 5 drops lavender

**Use:** Apply to itchy areas as needed

**Shelf life:** 6–9 months

---

### Warming Muscle Salve

**Best for:** Muscle tension, soreness, joint stiffness

**Making:**
1. Infuse 1 cup olive oil with:
   - ½ oz dried [[Rosemary]]
   - ½ oz dried [[Ginger]] (if growing, or dried)
   - ¼ oz dried [[Cayenne]] pepper (if comfortable handling)
   4 weeks

2. Make salve: 1 cup infused oil + 1 oz beeswax

3. Optional: Add warming essential oil (black pepper, ginger, or eucalyptus)

**Use:** Massage into sore muscles/joints 2–3x daily

**Caution:** Test on small area first (warming herbs may cause slight warming sensation)

**Shelf life:** 6–9 months

## Salve Consistency Guide

| Consistency | Ratio | Best For | Application |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Very soft (almost like thick oil)** | 5:1 oil:wax | Massage, large areas | Rubs on easily, spreads far |
| **Soft/spreadable (butter-like)** | 4:1 oil:wax | Most wounds, daily use | Ideal for most purposes |
| **Medium (holds shape)** | 3:1 oil:wax | Targeted application, lips | Stays where applied |
| **Firm (waxy)** | 2:1 oil:wax | Lip balms, difficult areas | Lasts longer on skin |
| **Very firm** | 1:1 oil:wax | Extreme durability | Difficult to apply, very waxy |

## Essential Oil Additions (Optional)

**If adding essential oils to finished salve:**

1. Let salve cool to about 140°F (still pourable, not hot)
2. Add essential oil: 10–20 drops per 1 oz salve
3. Stir thoroughly
4. Pour into containers
5. Let cool completely before capping

**Common additions:**
- **Lavender (5 drops):** Extra soothing, pleasant scent
- **Chamomile (5 drops):** Calming, anti-inflammatory
- **Eucalyptus (3 drops):** respiratory opening (not on wounds)
- **Tea Tree (2–3 drops):** Antimicrobial (use cautiously, can irritate)

## Storage & Shelf Life

### Ideal Storage
- **Temperature:** Cool (60–75°F), stable
- **Light:** Cool, dark place (avoid heat/direct sun)
- **Container:** Glass jars or metal tins (not plastic)
- **Seal:** Tight-fitting lids

### How to Tell When Salve Has Gone Bad
- ✗ Moldy appearance or smell
- ✗ Rancid oil smell (gone off)
- ✗ Separated into layers
- ✗ Unusual color change

**Safe to use:**
- ✓ Slight color variation (normal)
- ✓ Powder on top (beeswax can separate; stir to combine)
- ✓ Slightly harder with age (normal)

### Shelf Life
- **Without vitamin E:** 6–9 months
- **With vitamin E oil:** 9–12 months
- **Refrigerated:** Up to 18 months

## Application Guide

### For Wounds
1. Clean area gently
2. Pat dry with sterile cloth
3. Apply thin layer of salve
4. Can cover with sterile gauze or bandage
5. Reapply 2–3x daily

### For General Skin
1. Clean hands/area
2. Apply small amount salve
3. Rub gently into skin
4. Wash hands after (unless treating hands)

### For Lips
1. Apply small amount
2. Rub in gently
3. Reapply as needed throughout day

### For Muscle/Joint
1. Apply generous amount to affected area
2. Massage gently but firmly
3. Can cover with warm cloth to retain warmth/moisture
4. Wash hands after application
5. Use 2–3x daily

## Common Issues

| Problem | Cause | Solution |
|---|---|---|
| **Too soft/oily** | Too much oil, not enough wax | Add more beeswax (melt with small amount old salve) |
| **Too hard/waxy** | Too much wax, not enough oil | Add more oil (warm both together) |
| **Grainy texture** | Wax cooled unevenly | Reheat and scoop into smooth container |
| **Won't solidify** | Oil temperature too low when cooling | Reheat higher, ensure consistent cooling |
| **Separated into layers** | Ingredients didn't blend well | Reheat and stir more thoroughly |
| **White powder on top** | Beeswax separation (normal) | Stir back in; doesn't affect quality |
| **Grainy after months** | Natural wax separation | Stir or reheat; doesn't indicate spoilage |
