# 🥤 Syrup Guide

A complete guide to making and using herbal syrups, sweet preparations that preserve herbs and make medicine palatable.

---

## What is an Herbal Syrup?

An herbal syrup is a concentrated herbal preparation combined with sweetener (honey, sugar, or glycerin), creating a thick, spoon-able medicine that's easier to take than tea, especially for children.

**Advantages:**
- Taste-masked (sweet covers herbal bitterness)
- Easy for children to take
- Concentrated (high herbal content)
- Good shelf life (several months refrigerated)
- Spoon-able dosing (easier than tea)

**Best for:** 
- Respiratory support (cough syrups)
- Digestive support (after-meal remedy)
- Getting children to take medicine

**Not ideal for:**
- Long-term storage (3–5 years like tinctures)
- Diabetic individuals (sugar content)
- Very heat-sensitive herbs

---

## Basic Syrup Formula

### Simple Ratio

**1 cup strong herbal infusion + 2 tbsp–½ cup sweetener**

---

## How Sweetener Works

**Sugar/Honey Role:**
- Preserves (osmotic effect draws water from bacteria)
- Masks bitter taste
- Creates proper consistency
- Adds energy + nourishment

**Sweetener Options:**
- **Honey:** Best (antimicrobial, nourishing, medicinal)
- **Sugar (cane):** Traditional, good preservation
- **Maple syrup:** Milder taste, more glycemic
- **Agave:** Mild, less crystallization
- **Glycerin:** For non-sugar option (less preservative)

## Syrup Making: Step-by-Step

### Method 1: Herbal Tea Syrup

**Time:** 30 minutes (fastest method)

#### Ingredients
- 1 cup strong herbal infusion
- 2–4 tbsp honey (or up to ½ cup for thicker syrup)
- Optional: 1 tbsp apple cider vinegar (enhances preservation, flavor)

#### Process

**Step 1: Make Strong Infusion**
1. Brew strong herbal tea: 2–3 tbsp herb per 8 oz water
2. Steep 15–20 minutes (longer = more extraction)
3. Strain thoroughly

**Step 2: Combine with Sweetener**
1. Heat infusion gently to warm (not boiling, which harms honey)
2. Add honey while warm (dissolves easier)
3. Stir until completely dissolved
4. Optional: Add apple cider vinegar

**Step 3: Cool & Bottle**
1. Cool to room temperature
2. Pour into glass bottles with tight lid
3. Label with herb name(s), date, suggested use

#### Shelf Life
3–4 months refrigerated (honey's antimicrobial action)

---

### Method 2: Decoction Syrup (Stronger)

**For roots, bark, tougher herbs requiring simmering**

#### Ingredients
- 1 cup strong herbal decoction (simmered)
- 3–4 tbsp honey

#### Process

**Step 1: Make Decoction**
1. Use roots/barks: [[Horehound]], [[Licorice]] (if growing), dried ginger
2. Simmer in water 20–30 minutes
3. Strain thoroughly, measure 1 cup liquid remaining

**Step 2: Reduce (Optional but Recommended)**
1. Pour liquid into pot
2. Simmer gently until reduced to ¾ cup
3. This concentrates herbs + helps honey preservation

**Step 3: Add Honey**
1. Cool slightly
2. Add 3–4 tbsp honey, stir until dissolved

**Step 4: Bottle**
1. Pour into glass bottles
2. Label, refrigerate

#### Shelf Life
3–4 months refrigerated

## Popular Herbal Syrups

### Classic Cough Syrup

**Best for:** Cough, respiratory comfort, especially nighttime

**Ingredients:**
- 1 tbsp [[Thyme]]
- 1 tbsp [[Horehound]]
- ½ tbsp [[Ginger]] (dried, from spice cupboard)
- 1 cup water

**Making:**
1. Decoction: Simmer herbs 20 minutes in 1 cup water
2. Strain, measure 1 cup liquid
3. Add 4 tbsp honey (thick consistency for cough comfort)
4. Optional: Add 1 tbsp apple cider vinegar (enhances)
5. Cool, bottle, refrigerate

**Dose:** 1 tsp every 2–4 hours as needed for cough

**Use:** Best bedtime before sleep

**Shelf life:** 3–4 months refrigerated

---

### Digestive Sweet Syrup

**Best for:** After meals, digestive support, pleasant flavor

**Ingredients:**
- 1 tbsp [[Fennel]] seed
- 1 tbsp [[Ginger]] (dried)
- ½ tbsp [[Lemon Balm]]
- 1 cup water

**Making:**
1. Infusion: Steep lighter herbs ([[Lemon Balm]]) in hot water first (10 min)
2. Add hardier herbs early: Simmer [[Fennel]] + [[Ginger]]15 minutes more
3. Strain, collect 1 cup
4. Add 3 tbsp honey
5. Cool, bottle, refrigerate

**Dose:** 1 tsp after meals

**Taste:** Warm, sweet, digestive-pleasant

**Shelf life:** 3–4 months refrigerated

---

### Nervine Calming Syrup

**Best for:** Bedtime, stress support, gentle action

**Ingredients:**
- 1 tbsp dried [[Lavender]]
- ½ tbsp dried [[Lemon Balm]]
- ½ tbsp dried [[Chamomile]]
- 1 cup water

**Making:**
1. Infusion: Steep all herbs in hot water 15–20 minutes
2. Strain, collect 1 cup
3. Add 3 tbsp honey
4. Cool, bottle, refrigerate

**Dose:** 1 tsp before bed or 2–3x daily during stress

**Taste:** Floral, sweet, calming-pleasant

**Shelf life:** 3–4 months refrigerated

---

### Immune Support Syrup

**Best for:** General immune support, especially children

**Ingredients:**
- 1 tbsp dried [[Echinacea]] root (or leaf if root unavailable)
- ½ tbsp dried [[Thyme]]
- ½ tbsp [[Ginger]]
- 1 cup water

**Making:**
1. Decoction: Simmer all herbs 20 minutes
2. Strain, collect 1 cup
3. Add 4 tbsp honey
4. Optional: Add 1 tbsp apple cider vinegar
5. Cool, bottle, refrigerate

**Dose:** 1 tsp, 2–3x daily during cold season, or at first symptoms

**For children:** 1 tsp per year of age, 1–2x daily

**Shelf life:** 3–4 months refrigerated

## Syrup Consistency Guide

| Consistency | Honey/Sugar Amount | Best For | Application |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Thin (like maple syrup)** | 2–3 tbsp per cup infusion | Drizzle, mix into food | Easier to pour |
| **Medium (like honey)** | 3–4 tbsp per cup infusion | Spoon, coating throat | Ideal for cough |
| **Thick (like molasses)** | 4–6 tbsp per cup infusion | Slow drip, adheres well | Maximum medicinal feel |
| **Extra-thick (paste-like)** | More heating, some reduction | Lozenge-like consistency | Effort to consume |

## Storage & Shelf Life

### Ideal Storage
- **Refrigerator:** Cool, dark shelf (preferred)
- **Cool cupboard:** If airtight container, short-term
- **NOT room temperature:** Accelerates spoilage

### Signs of Good Syrup
✓ Nice color (darker/herb-hued)  
✓ No mold
✓ Smooth consistency  
✓ Pleasant herbal smell  
✓ No separation  

### Signs of Spoilage
✗ Mold (blue/white/fuzzy)  
✗ Fermented smell (yeasty, off)  
✗ Separation into liquid + solids  
✗ Crystallization (very old honey)  

**If moldy:** Discard entirely (don't attempt to salvage)

### Shelf Life by Storage Method
- **Refrigerated, honey-based:** 3–4 months
- **Refrigerated, sugar-based:** 2–3 months  
- **Refrigerated, glycerin-based:** 1–2 months
- **Frozen:** 6+ months

---

## Dosage Guide

### Children
- **Under 1 year:** Consult healthcare provider (honey caution < 1 yr)
- **1–3 years:** ¼ tsp, 1–2x daily
- **4–6 years:** ½ tsp, 1–2x daily
- **7–12 years:** 1 tsp, 1–2x daily
- **Adolescent/adult:** 1 tsp, 1–3x daily

### Adults
- **Maintenance:** 1 tsp, 1x daily
- **Therapeutic:** 1 tsp, 2–3x daily
- **Acute cough:** 1 tsp every 2–4 hours as needed

### Application Methods
- **Direct:** Spoon directly in mouth
- **In water:** Dissolve in warm water, drink as "medicine tea"
- **On toast:** Spread like jam (pleasant for children)
- **In warm milk:** Mix for bedtime drink
- **On oatmeal:** Drizzle for breakfast

## Making Thicker Syrup (Oil Infusion Instead)

**Alternative method for longer shelf life:**

1. Make herbal-infused oil (4-week process)
2. Strain thoroughly
3. Infuse this oil with honey in 1:1 ratio (instead of water)
4. Never heat honey above 120°F (damages medicinal compounds)
5. Shelf life extends to 6+ months refrigerated

---

## Honey Syrup Advantages

**Why honey is best sweetener for syrups:**

✓ Natural antimicrobial (preserves longer)  
✓ Medicinal in itself (cough soothing, throat coating)  
✓ Contains enzymes (survived extraction methods)  
✓ Doesn't crystallize like sugar  
✓ Works at lower temps (preserves herbs better)  
✓ Pairs well with herbal flavors  

**Cautions:**
- Don't heat honey above 120°F (kills enzymes)
- Not for infants under 1 year (botulism risk)
- Check honey source (raw better than refined)

## Glycerin-Based Syrup (Alcohol-Free Alternative)

**Best for:** No honey desire, vegan preference

### Recipe

**Ingredients:**
- 1 cup strong herbal infusion or decoction
- ½–¾ cup vegetable glycerin

**Process:**
1. Make cool or warm infusion (decoction for roots)
2. Cool completely
3. Mix 1 cup infusion + ½ cup glycerin
4. Stir thoroughly (glycerin is viscous)
5. Bottle, label, refrigerate

**Dose:** Same as honey syrup (1 tsp)

**Taste:** Sweet, less herbal than honey version

**Shelf life:** 1–2 months refrigerated (less preservative than honey)

## Syrup Troubleshooting

| Problem | Cause | Prevention/Fix |
|---|---|---|
| **Too thin (watery)** | Not enough honey/sweetener | Add more honey (reheat, stir in) |
| **Too thick (difficult to spoon)** | Too much honey, or reduction | Add small amount infusion |
| **Crystallized solid** | Very old honey crystallizing (normal) | Warm gently to dissolve, stir |
| **Moldy** | Contamination or low sugar | Discard; ensure clean bottles, good ratio next time |
| **Separated liquid + settled solids** | Honey + herb not blended well | Shake before use; use less herb material |
| **Fermented smell** | Bacterial growth (low honey %) | Discard; increase honey:infusion ratio |
