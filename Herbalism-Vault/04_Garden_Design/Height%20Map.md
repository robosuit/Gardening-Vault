# 📏 Garden Height Map

Use this guide to arrange herbs by mature height, creating visually pleasing beds and ensuring proper sun access to all plants.

---

## Garden Height Layering (Front to Back)

Arrange plants with shortest in front, tallest in back, to prevent shading smaller plants.

---

## 🌱 Front Border (0–18 inches)

**Best for:** Edging garden beds, container planting, visual foreground

### [[Thyme]]
- Height: 6–12 inches
- Spread: 12–18 inches
- Sun: Full sun
- Water: Low
- Best placement: Along bed edge, pathway border
- Cascading over edges acceptable

### [[Oregano]]
- Height: 12–18 inches
- Spread: 18–24 inches
- Sun: Full sun
- Water: Low
- Best placement: Front but not extreme edge (spreads wide)

### [[Chamomile]]
- Height: 12–24 inches
- Spread: 12–18 inches
- Sun: Full sun to partial shade
- Water: Medium
- Best placement: Front of bed, can soften hard edges
- Note: Tends to self-seed; embrace or deadhead accordingly

### [[Marjoram]]
- Height: 12–18 inches
- Spread: 18–24 inches
- Sun: Full sun
- Water: Low to medium
- Best placement: Front-middle, not extreme edge

---

## 🌿 Middle Layer (18–36 inches)

**Best for:** Central bed placement, supporting cast of garden

### [[Lavender]]
- Height: 18–24 inches (can reach 36")
- Spread: 18–30 inches
- Sun: Full sun
- Water: Low
- Best placement: Middle, grouping 3–5 together for impact
- Variety note: Compact cultivars available (12–18")

### [[Bergamot]]
- Height: 24–36 inches
- Spread: 24–36 inches
- Sun: Full sun to partial shade
- Water: Medium to moist
- Best placement: Middle layer
- Social: Magnet for pollinators; notice increased bee activity

### [[Hyssop]]
- Height: 18–24 inches (can reach 32")
- Spread: 12–18 inches (narrow)
- Sun: Full sun
- Water: Low to medium
- Best placement: Middle, upright form nice for tight spaces

### [[Sage]]
- Height: 18–30 inches
- Spread: 18–30 inches
- Sun: Full sun
- Water: Low
- Best placement: Middle, sculptural form adds interest
- Note: Gets woody with age; prune hard in spring

### [[Lemon Balm]]
- Height: 24–36 inches
- Spread: 24–30 inches
- Sun: Full sun to partial shade
- Water: Medium
- Best placement: Middle, but contained (can spread aggressively)
- Caution: Mint family; needs containment strategy

### [[Catnip]]
- Height: 24–36 inches
- Spread: 18–24 inches
- Sun: Full sun
- Water: Low to medium
- Best placement: Middle, consider cat visitor impacts
- Note: Cats may damage plants; plant away from seating

---

## 🌾 Tall Layer (3–6 feet)

**Best for:** Back of bed, focal points, wind breaks

### [[Lovage]]
- Height: 4–6 feet
- Spread: 2–3 feet
- Sun: Full sun to partial shade
- Water: Medium (prefers consistent)
- Best placement: Back, tall focal point
- Impact: Dramatic height, hollow stems interesting
- Note: May need staking in windy spots

### [[Fennel]]
- Height: 3–5 feet
- Spread: 18–24 inches
- Sun: Full sun
- Water: Low to medium
- Best placement: Back, tall but narrow
- Uses: Culinary, traditional, attracts beneficial insects
- Caution: Allelopathic (may suppress nearby plants); give space

### [[Dill]]
- Height: 2–4 feet (varieties vary)
- Spread: 12–18 inches
- Sun: Full sun
- Water: Medium
- Best placement: Back or middle
- Note: Self-seeds readily; mark/manage accordingly
- Benefit: Attracts parasitic wasps (beneficial insects)

### [[Echinacea]]
- Height: 2–4 feet
- Spread: 18–24 inches
- Sun: Full sun
- Water: Low to medium
- Best placement: Back to middle
- Impact: Flowers on tall stems, pollinator magnet
- Blooms: Midsummer to fall

---

## 🌳 Very Tall Plants (5–8 feet, Use Sparingly)

### [[Horehound]]
- Height: 2–3 feet (less tall, but medicinal)
- Spread: 18–24 inches
- Sun: Full sun
- Water: Low
- Best placement: Tall middle or back

### [[Peppermint]] / [[Mint Family]]
- Height: 2–4 feet (if trained)
- Spread: Extremely aggressive (underground runners)
- Sun: Partial shade okay
- Water: Moist
- **CRITICAL:** Plant in containers only, or use underground barriers
- Best placement: Contained area, not open bed

## 📐 Sample Garden Layouts

### Small Standard Bed (4' x 8')

```
BACK
[Lovage]  [Fennel]  [Dill]
[Lavender]  [Bergamot]  [Sage]
[Thyme]  [Oregano]  [Chamomile]
FRONT
```

**Sun Access:** Taller plants don't shade shorter ones
**Spacing:** Accounts for mature spread
**Watering:** Grouped by similar needs (left: low-water; center: medium)

---

### Medium Pollinator Bed (6' x 12')

```
BACK (NORTH FACING)
[Dill] [Echinacea] [Fennel] [Lovage]

MID
[Bergamot]  [Lavender]  [Lavender]  [Sage]

FRONT (SOUTH FACING)  
[Thyme]  [Oregano]  [Chamomile]  [Marjoram]
```

**Total height range:** 6" (thyme) to 6' (lovage)
**Bloom sequence:** Spring through fall
**Pollinator value:** High (flowers at various heights all season)

---

### Vertical Container Stacking (Small Space)

**Use tiered containers:**
1. **Back (tall container, raised):** Lovage or Dill
2. **Center (medium container):** Lavender + Bergamot
3. **Front (low container):** Thyme + Oregano edge

**Benefits:**
- Maximizes space
- Improved drainage
- Easier harvesting
- Year-round portability

---

## View Lines Considerations

### Sightline Height
- **Standing eye level:** ~5'–6' (most people)
- **Plant tall herbs behind sitting areas** (to not obstruct view)
- **Plant shorter herbs in front of seating** (create frame, don't block sightline)

### Visual Impact
- Odd-numbered groupings (3, 5, 7) are more visually appealing than even
- Repetition: Plant same herb 3+ places for cohesion
- Contrast: Tall + short near each other for interest

## Preventing Shade Problems

### Shade-Causing Mistakes
✗ Planting tall (Fennel, Lovage) in front of shorter (Thyme, Oregano)  
✗ Planting wide-spreading (Bergamot) too close to compact (Sage)  
✗ Not removing dead tall stems early spring (blocks spring growth)  
✗ Letting self-seeders (Dill, Fennel) spread indiscriminately  

### Preventing Shade
✓ Arrange front → back by height consistently  
✓ Prune taller plants in early spring for cleaner emergence  
✓ Thin Dill/Fennel seedlings or pull extras  
✓ Allow ample spacing (don't overcrowd)  
✓ Monitor and adjust first year (many plants different than expected)  

---

## Seasonal Height Changes

**Spring:** Plants emerge different heights than summer (growth continues)

**Early Summer:** Most reach mature height by June

**Late Summer:** Some taller plants may lean/sprawl (staking may help)

**Fall:** Many perennials shorten as they prepare dormancy

**Winter:** Cut back hard; minimal height

---

## First-Year Adjustment

**Note:** Newly planted herbs often grow differently than expected:
- May stay shorter first year
- May grow faster than expected
- May spread wider or narrower
- Perennials take 2–3 years to mature size

**Recommendation:** Don't be afraid to adjust spacing year 2 based on actual growth patterns.
