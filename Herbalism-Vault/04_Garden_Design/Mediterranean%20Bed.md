# 🌿 Mediterranean Herb Bed Design

A complete garden bed layout for heat-loving, drought-tolerant Mediterranean herbs that thrive together with minimal maintenance.

---

## What is a Mediterranean Herb Garden?

A Mediterranean bed focuses on herbs originating from dry, sunny regions around the Mediterranean Sea. These herbs share:
- ✓ Preference for full sun (6+ hours)
- ✓ Drought tolerance (low water needs)
- ✓ Excellent drainage requirements
- ✓ Lean soil preference (avoid over-fertilizing)
- ✓ Similar harvesting/uses (culinary + medicinal)
- ✓ Similar pest/disease resistance
- ✓ Aromatic foliage (volatile oil rich)

**Benefits:** Low-maintenance, pest-resistant, abundant harvest, beautiful/fragrant

---

## Core Mediterranean Herbs

### [[Thyme]]
- Height: 6–12 inches
- Spread: 12–18 inches
- Placement: Front border (cascading over edge nice)
- Water: Very low (once established)
- Propagation: Cuttings easily
- Culinary: Essential for Mediterranean cooking

### [[Oregano]]
- Height: 12–18 inches
- Spread: 18–24 inches
- Placement: Front-middle
- Water: Low
- Propagation: Division in spring, cuttings
- Culinary: Core Mediterranean herb; essential

### [[Rosemary]]
- Height: 24–36 inches (varies by form)
- Spread: 18–30 inches
- Placement: Middle to back (depending on variety)
- Water: Low (very drought tolerant established)
- Propagation: Cuttings (slow rooting, 4–8 weeks)
- Culinary: Bold flavor, medicinal value
- Tender: Zone 7B = borderline hardy; protect in winter
- Varieties: 'Tuscan Blue' (upright), 'Prostrate' (ground cover)

### [[Sage]]
- Height: 18–30 inches
- Spread: 18–30 inches
- Placement: Middle
- Water: Low
- Propagation: Cuttings, division
- Culinary: Savory, traditional pairing with rosemary
- Forms: Green sage (standard), Red sage, Gold sage (color varieties)

### [[Marjoram]]
- Height: 12–18 inches
- Spread: 18–24 inches
- Placement: Front-middle
- Water: Low to medium
- Propagation: Cuttings, division
- Culinary: Milder than oregano, sweet undertone
- Note: Often confused with oregano but distinct flavor

### [[Hyssop]] *(Optional, slightly less Mediterranean)*
- Height: 18–24 inches
- Spread: 12–18 inches
- Placement: Middle
- Water: Low to medium
- Medicinal: Respiratory support
- Culinary: Unique flavor, attractive flowers

## 📐 Sample Mediterranean Bed Designs

### Small Mediterranean Spiral (3' diameter)

```
            [Rosemary-tall]
        [Sage]      [Oregano]
    [Thyme]     [Marjoram]
[Thyme edge] [Thyme edge]
```

**Placement:** Herb spiral (height increases toward center)
**Size:** ~3–4 square feet
**Plants needed:** 7–8 plants (2–3 of each thyme)
**Full sun:** 6+ hours essential
**Soil:** Add perlite/sand for drainage

---

### Standard Rectangle Bed (4' x 8')

```
BACK (NORTH)
[Rosemary (3ft)] [Sage] [Oregano]

MIDDLE
[Thyme]  [Marjoram]  [Thyme]  [Hyssop]

FRONT (SOUTH)
[Thyme-cascading] [Thyme-cascading] [Marjoram-edge]
```

**Total plants:** 11–13 (adjust spacing for mature size)
**Sun exposure:** South-facing bed ideal
**Drainage:** Crucial; raise bed or add drainage amends

---

### Container Mediterranean Mix

**For patios, balconies, limited space:**

**Large Container (14+ inches):**
- Center: 1 Rosemary or Sage
- Edge: 2–3 Thyme (cascading over sides)
- Filler: Marjoram

**Medium Container (10–12 inches):**
- 1 Oregano or Thyme (single variety)

**Drainage:** Crucial; use quality potting soil, not garden soil

## Soil Preparation (Critical for Mediterranean Success)

### Before Planting

**Step 1: Test Drainage**
1. Dig hole, fill with water
2. If water drains in <2 minutes = good
3. If water pools = needs amendment

**Step 2: Amend Soil** (if needed)
- Add 1/3 perlite or coarse sand to existing soil
- Till in 2–3 inches deep
- Creates loose, draining medium

**Step 3: Avoid Rich Soil**
- Traditional garden compost = TOO rich for Mediterranean herbs
- Lean, poor soil = stronger flavor, more compact growth
- Skip adding fertilizer (especially nitrogen)

**Step 4: Ensure pH Neutral (6.0–7.5)**
- Most Mediterranean herbs prefer near-neutral
- Thyme/Oregano tolerate slightly acidic
- Test kit available at garden centers

---

## Spacing for Mature Size

**When Planting, Space for Mature Spread:**

- **Thyme:** 12–18 inches apart
- **Oregano:** 18–24 inches apart
- **Rosemary:** 24–30 inches apart
- **Sage:** 18–30 inches apart (depending on form)
- **Marjoram:** 18–24 inches apart
- **Hyssop:** 18–24 inches apart

**Avoid:** Overcrowding (encourages fungal issues, poor air circulation)

---

## Planting Timeline (Zone 7B)

### Spring (April–May)
- Best time to plant most Mediterranean herbs
- After last frost (April 15)
- Soil workable, moderate temps
- Plant = established by summer

### Early Fall (August–September)
- Secondary planting time
- Cooler temps help establishment
- Less watering needed
- Plant by September 15 (enough time before winter)

### Avoid Winter* *(December–February)*
- Root establishment minimal in dormancy
- Winter kill risk

---

## Watering & Establishment

### First 4 Weeks (Establishment)
- Water 2–3x per week (keep soil moist, not soggy)
- Check soil: Top inch dry = water needed
- No fertilizer (concentrate on root growth)
- Mulch 2–3 inches around plants, away from stems

### Months 2–3
- Reduce frequency (1–2x per week)
- Plant roots growing deeper
- Begin reducing (Mediterranean plants adapt to dryness)

### After 3 Months (Established)
- Water minimal: 1x weekly or less
- Rely on rainfall
- Only water during extended drought
- Over-watering = root rot
- Withering leaves = likely over-watering, not under

---

## Mulching Mediterranean Beds

### Best Mulches
- **Pea gravel:** Mediterranean aesthetic, good drainage
- **Wood chips:** Traditional, breaks down into soil
- **Aged bark:** Similar to wood chips
- **Straw:** Good, breaks down fast

### NOT Recommended
✗ Landscape fabric (blocks air, water; Mediterranean plants hate)  
✗ Fresh bark (decomposes too quickly, depletes nitrogen)  
✗ Thick mulch (Mediterranean plants prefer "breathable" conditions)  

### Amount
- 2–3 inches maximum
- Keep 1–2 inches away from stems (prevent rot)
- Thin/light application (not heavy)

---

## Pruning & Maintenance

### Spring (March–April)
- Prune hard (remove 1/3 of growth)
- Stimulates bushy, compact growth
- Remove any winter-killed stems
- Timing: Before new growth starts

### Summer (June–August)
- Harvest regularly (acts as pruning)
- Pinch tops to encourage branching
- Remove flowers if foliage priority
- Heat-tolerant; little care needed

### Fall (September–October)
- Light deadheading (spent flowers)
- Don't hard prune (newly growing plants need structure)
- Let some flowers set seed (nature's plan)

### Winter (November–February)
- Minimal pruning (dormant = vulnerable)
- Rosemary/tender herbs: Mulch heavily or container move
- Remove dead wood in late winter (preparatory)

---

## Harvest & Use

### Harvesting Guidelines
- Harvest regularly = fuller, bushier plants
- Cut top 1/3 of growth
- Leave bottom structure intact
- Morning harvest (post-dew) = peak flavor
- Dry herbs for winter use

### Culinary Combinations
- **Thyme + Rosemary:** Classic savory duo
- **Thyme + Oregano + Marjoram:** Herb blend for Italian cooking
- **Sage + Rosemary:** Pairing for poultry
- **All together:** Mediterranean pizza/pasta herb mix

### Medicinal Uses
- **Thyme:** Respiratory, antimicrobial
- **Oregano:** Antimicrobial, antioxidant, digestive
- **Rosemary:** Circulation, memory support, antioxidant
- **Sage:** Throat, digestion, antimicrobial
- **Marjoram:** Digestive, relaxing, warming

---

## Companion Planting in Mediterranean Bed

### Beneficial Companions
- **Lavender:** Pairs beautifully, similar needs (add for color)
- **Thyme + Oregano:** Natural pairing, no conflict
- **Sage + Rosemary:** Both prefer dry, love full sun

### Avoid Mixing
✗ Mints (too aggressive, will overtake)  
✗ Very moisture-loving herbs (they'll compete for different water)  
✗ Shade-preferring herbs (wrong light needs)

## Problem Solving

### Common Issues

**Powdery Mildew (white coating on leaves)**
- Cause: Too much moisture, poor air circulation
- Prevention: Space plants wide, water at soil level (not foliage), ensure drainage
- Solution: Remove affected leaves, improve circulation, reduce watering

**Root Rot (plants dying despite moisture)**
- Cause: Over-watering, poor drainage
- Prevention: Ensure well-draining soil, water sparingly
- Solution: Reduce watering immediately; may be too late to save

**Leggy Growth (sparse, tall, not bushy)**
- Cause: Insufficient light or over-fertilizing
- Prevention: Full sun location, avoid nitrogen fertilizer
- Solution: Prune hard in spring to force branching

**Pest Issues (rare but possible)**
- Spider mites, aphids: Uncommon on healthy Mediterranean herbs
- Prevention: Healthy plants resist; good drainage + air circulation
- Solution: Spray neem oil if needed; usually not necessary

## Year-Round Care Calendar

| Month | Task |
|---|---|
| **February** | Plan bed, order plants |
| **March** | Prepare soil, hard prune existing plants |
| **April** | Plant new stock, water regularly |
| **May** | Begin harvesting, reduce water |
| **June–August** | Harvest regularly, minimal watering |
| **September** | Light deadheading, prepare for dormancy |
| **October** | Reduce water, mulch tender plants |
| **November–January** | Minimal care, plan next year |
