# 🌿 April Planting Guide (Zone 7B)

**Season:** Spring in Full Force  
**Average Temps:** 55–65°F (warming rapidly)  
**Days of Light:** Long (12–14 hours)  
**Frost Risk:** LAST FROST April 5–15; diminishing rapidly  
**Soil:** Warm, workable, ideal for planting

---

## This Month's Role

April is **transplant month**. Move indoor seedlings (started Feb–March) to the garden after last frost. Direct sow warm-weather herbs. This is peak planting action.

---

## 📅 Timing: Last Frost

**Zone 7B Last Frost Date: April 5–15 (mid-month typical)**

### Watch Weather!
- Check local forecast mid-April
- Wait until nighttime temps consistently above 50°F
- Cold snap coming? Hold off planting tender herbs
- Clear week predicted? Plant day of last frost or after

### Transplanting Process

**Before transplanting:**
1. Harden off seedlings (acclimatize to sun/wind gradually)
   - Week of April 5–12: Part shade, sheltered
   - Week of April 12–19: Increasing sun exposure

2. Check seedling condition:
   - Healthy green leaves
   - No fungal issues
   - Well-developed roots (visible at soil surface okay)
   - 4–6 weeks old typically (ready)

**Transplanting:**

1. **Prepare planting holes** slightly larger than root ball
2. **Gently remove** seedling from tray (squeeze sides gently)
3. **Loosen roots** gently with fingers (break up root ball slightly)
4. **Plant at same depth** (bury to first leaves, not deeper)
5. **Backfill** with garden soil
6. **Water gently** immediately after transplanting
7. **Mulch** 1–2 inches around (away from stem)

## 🌼 Direct Sow Warm-Weather Herbs

**Once soil is warm (April 15+) and frost danger passes:**

### [[Anise]]
- **Sow:** Mid-late April (wait for soil warmth)
- **Depth:** ¼–½ inch
- **Spacing:** Thin to 12 inches apart
- **Germination:** 14–21 days
- **Notes:** Annual; plant once per year
- **Harvest:** August–September

### [[Borage]]
- **Sow:** Mid-late April (soil warmth needed)
- **Depth:** ½ inch
- **Spacing:** Thin to 18–24 inches apart
- **Germination:** 5–10 days (fast!)
- **Notes:** Self-sows readily; tall, spreading
- **Flowers:** Attract bees heavily

### [[Dill]]
- **Sow:** Mid-late April (first of several sowings)
- **Depth:** ¼ inch
- **Spacing:** Thin to 12 inches apart
- **Germination:** 7–10 days
- **Succession:** Sow again every 2–3 weeks through June
- **Notes:** Self-sows; you'll have volunteers
- **Harvest:** Summer through fall

### [[Fennel]]
- **Sow:** Mid-late April (once warm)
- **Depth:** Surface to light covering
- **Spacing:** Thin to 18–24 inches apart
- **Germination:** 10–14 days
- **Notes:** Biennial; persists; allelopathic (gives space)
- **Harvest:** Seed in late summer year 1 or year 2

## 🌿 Final Planting Tasks

### Beds Ready?
- [ ] Last frost date passed (check calendar)
- [ ] Soil warm (70°F+ for warm-weather herbs)
- [ ] Beds prepared + amended
- [ ] Spacing planned

### Plant Tender Herbs Last
**Plant in this order:**
1. Frost-hardy perennials first (Thistle, Echinacea, Lemon Balm)
2. Cool-tolerant annuals next (Cilantro already planted in March; can add more)
3. Warm-soil lovers last (Basil varieties - wait until late April/early May for soil warmth)

### Basil Exception
**[[Basil]] varieties need WARMTH:**
- Don't plant until late April or early May
- Soil should be 70°F+ (warmer, better)
- Basil in cold soil = rot, slow growth
- Cold-tolerant perennials: Plant earlier

## 🌾 Garden Care This Month

### Daily Checks
- [ ] Water transplants well (first week especially)
- [ ] Check for wilting (indicates underwatering)
- [ ] Monitor soil moisture (top inch should be moist, not soggy)
- [ ] Watch for late frost warnings (protect if needed with frost cloth)

### Weekly Tasks
- [ ] Weed emerging weeds (easier now)
- [ ] Stake tall plants (Lovage, Dill if needed)
- [ ] Deadhead spring bulbs if any nearby
- [ ] Scout for pests (early intervention best)

### Succession Planting
- [ ] Cilantro: Plant second crop this month (every 2 weeks)
- [ ] Dill: First sowing; plan second in May

## 🌳 Hardening Off Guide (Late March/Early April)

| Timeline | Condition | Duration | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Week 1 (April 5–12)** | Shade, sheltered | 1–3 hours | Build tolerance slowly |
| **Week 2 (April 12–19)** | Dappled sun, protected | 4–6 hours | Gradually increase |
| **Week 3 (April 19–25)** | Morning sun, afternoon shade | Most of day | Nearly acclimated |
| **Week 4 (April 26–30)** | Full sun (if not tender) | All day | Ready to transplant |

## 🌡️ Temperature Awareness

### Frost Risk April Schedule

**Early April (1–10):**
- High frost risk
- Protect tender seedlings
- Frost cloth available
- Transplant only frost-hardy perennials

**Mid-April (10–15):**
- Frost risk declining
- Last frost date typically occurs
- Check local forecast
- Once clear, transplant more

**Late April (15–30):**
- Frost danger mostly past
- Warm-season seeds safe
- Basil can plant by late April/May 1
- Borage, Anise, Dill, Fennel safe

---

## 🚰 Watering in April

### Transplants
- **First week:** Daily watering (transplant shock = water stress)
- **Week 2+:** Every 2–3 days (soil should be moist, not soggy)
- **Monitor:** Top inch dry = water time

### Direct Sown Seeds
- **Until germination:** Keep moist (7–21 days depending on seed)
- **After germination:** Let top inch dry between waterings
- **Watch:** Too wet = rot

### Rainfall
- April typically gets adequate rain (check forecast)
- Supplement if dry spell appears
- Mulch helps retain moisture

## 💡 April Tips

| Tip | Why |
|---|---|
| **Wait for warmth** | Cold soil = seed rot; patience pays |
| **Stake tall growers** | April winds can topple Dill, Fennel |
| **Water deeply** | Encourages deep rooting |
| **Mulch heavily** | Conserves moisture, suppresses weeds |
| **Harden off fully** | Acclimatization prevents plant shock |
| **Keep records** | Track what planted where, when |
| **Mark everything** | Months later you'll forget what's what |
