# 🌸 February Planting Guide (Zone 7B)

**Season:** Early Spring Preparation  
**Average Low:** 35–45°F  
**Days of Light:** Growing (8–10 hours)  
**Frost Risk:** Still possible; protect tender starts

---

## This Month's Role

February is **preparation + early starts**. Outdoor planting is limited by cold/frost risk, but indoor seed starting begins for spring transplants.

---

## 🌱 Indoor Seed Starting

**Start Under Lights (4–6 weeks before transplant)**

### [[Lavender]]
- **Why now?** Slow germinator, needs longest cycle
- **Medium:** Seed starting mix, moist but not soggy
- **Light:** 14–16 hours daily under grow lights
- **Heat:** 65–70°F optimal (heating mat helpful)
- **Germination:** 14–21 days typical
- **Transplant outdoors:** Mid-May (after last frost)
- **Notes:** Lavender seed sometimes needs stratification (cold period); soak seeds in cool water 24 hours before planting

### [[Rosemary]]
- **Why now?** Very slow germinator, needs 6–8 weeks
- **Medium:** Seed starting mix, keep consistently moist
- **Light:** 14–16 hours daily
- **Heat:** 70–75°F (heating mat recommended)
- **Germination:** 21–30 days (sometimes longer)
- **Transplant:** June (full establishment after warmth)
- **Notes:** Rosemary germinates slowly but reliably if warm; patience needed

## 🔧 Preparation Tasks

### Clean & Organize Seed Arsenal
- [ ] Inventory seed: Check germination dates
- [ ] Discard old seeds (over 2–3 years)
- [ ] Order replacements (supply sourcing)
- [ ] Organize by month for easy reference

### Start Seeds Under Lights
- [ ] Set up grow light system (if not already established)
- [ ] Gather seed starting supplies:
  - Seed starting mix (sterile, peat/coco-based)
  - Seed trays or small pots
  - Grow lights (LED ideal)
  - Heat mat (optional but helpful)
  - Watering can with fine spray

### Prepare Garden Beds (March-spring)
- [ ] Check drainage (dig test hole, fill with water)
- [ ] Note problem areas (wet, compacted, poor soil)
- [ ] Plan amendments (perlite for drainage, compost)
- [ ] Order supplies as needed

### Get Starts Scouting
- [ ] Research local nurseries (their selection, hours)
- [ ] Check what's available (some plants hard to find)
- [ ] Order plants in advance (rare varieties may need pre-order)
- [ ] Plan budget (how many transplants to purchase vs. grow from seed?)

## 📋 Seed Starting Checklists

### Daily Tasks (Once lights started)
- [ ] Check soil moisture (keep moist, don't waterlog)
- [ ] Monitor temperature (maintain 65–75°F)
- [ ] Watch for first seedlings (check daily after germination day-range passes)
- [ ] Provide light 14–16 hours once sprouted

### Weekly Tasks
- [ ] Inspect for stretching (seedlings reaching for light = light too far)
- [ ] Rotate trays (ensures even light)
- [ ] Check for mold/fungal issues
- [ ] Make notes on germination rates (track for posterity)

## 🎯 Seed-Starting Success Tips

| Tip | Why | How |
|---|---|---|
| **Warmth** | Seeds germinate best warm | Use heat mat: 70–75°F for most herbs |
| **Moisture** | Too dry = no germination; Too wet = rot | Water from below (tray method) or light misting |
| **Light after sprouting** | Seedlings need light immediately | Lights on day of sprouting; within 1–2 days if you miss |
| **Proper distance** | Too far = stretching; Too close = burning | Lights 2–3 inches above seedlings, adjust as they grow |
| **Air circulation** | Prevents fungal issues | Small fan near (but not directly on) seedlings |
| **Sterile medium** | Prevents damp-off fungus | Use seed-starting mix, not garden soil |
