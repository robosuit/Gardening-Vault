# 🌱 March Planting Guide (Zone 7B)

**Season:** Spring Awakening  
**Average Temps:** 45–55°F (still cool)  
**Days of Light:** Growing (10–12 hours)  
**Frost Risk:** Still high (last frost mid-April)  
**Soil:** Warming, workable

---

## This Month's Role

March is **peak indoor seed starting month**. You'll start the majority of herb transplants indoors for May planting. You'll also direct sow cold-hardy herbs outdoors.

### Seed Starting Process

**Equipment:**
- Seed-starting mix (sterile, peat or coco-based)
- Seed trays, cell packs, or small pots (drainage crucial)
- Grow lights (LED, 14–16 inches above seedlings)
- Heat mat (65–70°F optimal for most)
- Watering can with fine rose (sprinkle not spray)
- Labels + permanent marker

**Steps:**

1. **Fill containers** with moist seed-starting mix (pre-wet it)

2. **Place seeds:**
   - Follow packet directions (some need light, cold treatment, etc.)
   - Most herbs: Light sprinkle of soil on top OR surface-sow (don't bury deep)
   - Pat soil gently

3. **Mist gently** with water (don't wash seeds away)

4. **Cover** with plastic dome or humidity mat (keeps moist during germination)

5. **Provide warmth:** Heat mat underneath improves germination by 30%

6. **Provide light:** Once sprouted (usually 5–14 days), remove cover + provide 14–16 hours light daily

7. **Keep moist:** Top inch should stay moist but not waterlogged

**Germination timeline:**
- Fast: Basilsgarlic, Marjoram (5–7 days)
- Medium: Lemon Balm, Oregano, Thyme (7–14 days)
- Slow: Echinacea, Hyssop (14–21 days)

## 🌿 Direct Sow Outdoors (Cold-Hardy)

**These herbs tolerate frost and can be sown directly in garden as soil becomes workable.**

### [[Caraway]]
- **Sow:** Once soil is workable (as soon as you can dig without it clumping)
- **Depth:** Scatter on surface, press gently, light soil covering
- **Spacing:** Thin seedlings to 12 inches apart later
- **Notes:** Biennial; will flower year 2
- **Germination:** 2–3 weeks typically

### [[Chamomile]]
- **Sow:** Direct sow as soon as soil workable
- **Depth:** Surface sow (needs light to germinate) OR very light soil covering
- **Spacing:** Thin to 12–18 inches apart
- **Notes:** Self-sows readily; you may see volunteers for years
- **Germination:** 7–14 days

### [[Cilantro]]
- **Sow:** As soon as soil workable
- **Depth:** ¼ inch
- **Spacing:** Thin to 6 inches apart
- **Notes:** Cool-season herb; prefers spring/fall not summer
- **Succession sow:** Every 2 weeks through mid-April for continuous harvest
- **Germination:** 7–14 days

### [[Dandelion]]
- **Sow:** Direct in garden
- **Depth:** Surface light covering
- **Spacing:** Eventually 12–18 inches
- **Notes:** Not traditional but medicinal + edible
- **Germination:** 7–14 days

## 🌿 Hardening Off Seedlings

**If you started seeds indoors in February:**

**Hardening off** = gradually acclimating indoor seedlings to outdoor conditions.

**Week 1 (late March):**
- Day 1–2: Place in shaded outdoor location (sheltered) for 1–2 hours
- Day 3–4: Same location, 2–3 hours
- Day 5–7: Increase to 4–6 hours, add partial sun

**Week 2 (early April):**
- Full days outdoors in partial shade
- Bring in before temps drop below 50°F at night

**Week 3 (mid-April):**
- Gradually increase sun exposure (morning sun first)
- Keep bringing in if frost threatening

**By late April:** Ready for transplanting after last frost

## 🚰 Watering Notes

### Indoor Seedlings
- Keep soil consistently moist (not waterlogged)
- Daily check needed (small pots dry quickly)
- Mist gently or water from below
- Reduce water if fungal issues appear (damping off)

### Direct-Sown Seeds
- Keep soil moist until germinated (7–14 days typically)
- After germination: Reduce to letting top inch dry slightly
- Don't over-water (causes rot)

### Perennials Breaking Dormancy
- Provide water as they emerge
- Natural rainfall usually sufficient
- Only water if extended dry period

## 💡 March Tips

| Tip | Reason |
|---|---|
| **Start seeds early** | Long grow cycle = transplants ready when frost passes |
| **Don't rush outdoor planting** | Frost still likely; seeds germinate faster in warming April |
| **Harden off gradually** | Acclimate seedlings to sun/wind slowly |
| **Direct sow multiple times** | Cilantro every 2 weeks = continuous supply |
| **Keep records** | Track seed viability for next year |
| **Clean tools** | Sanitize to prevent disease spread |
