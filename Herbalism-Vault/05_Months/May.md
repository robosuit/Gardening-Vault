# 🌞 May Planting Guide (Zone 7B)

**Season:** Spring to Summer Transition  
**Average Temps:** 65–75°F (consistently warm)  
**Days of Light:** Long (14–16 hours)  
**Frost Risk:** NONE (safe to plant anything)  
**Soil:** Warm (70°F+), perfect for all herbs

---

## This Month's Role

May is **warm-season planting + establishment month**. Plant basil and other heat-loving herbs. Succession sow cool-season herbs one final time. Begin regular harvesting. Watch first flowers appear.

---

## 🌿 Plant Warm-Season Herbs (Finally!)

**May is the month for [[Basil]] and true summer herbs.**

### [[Thai Basil]]
- **Plant:** Early May when soil is warm
- **Spacing:** 12–18 inches apart
- **Sun:** Full sun preferred
- **Water:** Regular (doesn't tolerate dryness)
- **Pinch:** Pinch tops regularly to encourage bushiness
- **Flowers:** Let some flower (attractive, edible)
- **Harvest:** August–September peak

### [[Italian Basil]]
- **Plant:** Early May when soil warm
- **Spacing:** 12–18 inches apart
- **Culinary:** Classic pesto basil
- **Water:** Regular, consistent
- **Pinch:** Top regularly for branching
- **Varieties:** "Genovese" classic, "Lettuce Leaf" large leaves

### [[Purple Basil]]
- **Plant:** Early May 
- **Spacing:** 12–18 inches apart
- **Visual:** Beautiful purple foliage/flowers
- **Taste:** Slightly spicier than green
- **Culinary:** Interesting in salads, preserved
- **Pinch:** For bushiness

### General Basil Tips
- **All prefer:** Warm soil (65–70°F minimum)
- **All need:** Full sun (6+ hours)
- **All want:** Regular water (don't dry out)
- **All should:** Be pinched regularly (pinch = bushier = more productive)

---

## 🌼 Succession Planting (Final Rounds)

### [[Cilantro]] - Last Good Sowing
- **Sow:** Early May (last good sowing before heat)
- **Spacing:** Thin to 6 inches
- **Germination:** 5–10 days
- **Notes:** Bolts in summer heat; sow early or wait for August resurgence
- **Harvest:** May–June before bolting

### [[Dill]] - Second/Third Sowing
- **Sow:** Every 2–3 weeks through May
- **Notes:** Multiple sowings = continuous supply
- **Germination:** 7–10 days
- **Harvest:** Staggered July–September

### [[Chamomile]] - Thin & Monitor
- **If planted March:** Thin seedlings to 12–18 inch spacing
- **Self-seeders:** Now obvious; thin or accept volunteers
- **Monitor:** Remove excess if overwhelming neighboring plants

## 🌿 Harvesting Guidelines This Month

### Light Harvesting (Perennials first year)
- Take top ¼–⅓ of growth
- Leave structure for ongoing vigor
- Stimulates bushiness
- Don't strip completely

### Aggressive Harvesting (Mints)
- Peppermint wants heavy harvesting
- Cut down to 6–12 inches
- Regrows quickly
- Multiple harvests per summer possible

### What NOT to harvest yet
- First-year Echinacea, Bergamot (let establish)
- Lovage (small plants; let grow)
- Rosemary (if young; wait until established)

## 🚰 Watering in May

### Warm Month = More Water Needed
- **Daily check** during dry weather
- **Every 2–3 days** typical watering schedule
- **Basil especially:** Consistent moisture needed
- **Drought-tolerant** (Thyme, Oregano): Less frequent

### Signs of Water Stress
- **Under-watering:** Wilting despite humid air, slow growth
- **Over-watering:** Yellowing, mushy stems, fungal smells

### Mulch Benefit
- 2–3 inches mulch: Conserves moisture 30–50%
- Keeps soil cooler (important for basil)
- Suppresses weeds
- Breaks down into soil organic matter
