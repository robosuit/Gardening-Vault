# 🍂 September Planting Guide (Zone 7B)

**Season:** Fall Transition  
**Average Temps:** 70–80°F (warm but cooling)  
**Days of Light:** Shortening (12–14 hours)  
**Frost Risk:** None yet (first frost Oct 25–Nov 5)  
**Soil:** Still warm, beginning to cool

---

## This Month's Role

September is **cool-season restart month**. Plant cilantro, dill, and other cool-season herbs one last time for fall harvest. Begin serious preservation (drying, tinctures) as summer annuals decline. Enjoy peak harvest of perennials.

---

## 🌿 Plant Cool-Season Herbs (Fall Crop)

**September is perfect for cool-season replanting.** Cool nights + warm days = ideal for these herbs.

### [[Cilantro]]
- **Sow:** Early to mid-September
- **Depth:** ¼ inch
- **Spacing:** Thin to 6 inches
- **Germination:** 7–14 days
- **Harvest window:** October–November before frost
- **Preference:** Much prefers cool weather to spring/summer
- **Notes:** This is actually better crop than spring

### [[Dill]]
- **Sow:** Early to mid-September
- **Depth:** ¼ inch
- **Spacing:** Thin to 12 inches
- **Germination:** 7–10 days
- **Harvest:** October–November
- **Fall advantage:** Slower bolting than summer

### [[Chamomile]]
- **Option 1:** Direct sow (may overwinter, flower next year)
- **Option 2:** Let existing plants self-seed
- **Notes:** Often self-seeds prolifically; accept volunteers
- **Harvest:** If flowering by November, use sparingly

### [[Dandelion]] (if growing)
- **Sow:** Early September
- **Notes:** Overwinters; you'll harvest spring
- **Advantage:** Spring greens from fall planting

## 🌾 Preservation Begins

**Summer is peak; time to preserve for winter.**

### Drying Herbs

**Best herbs to dry now:**
- **Oregano, Marjoram, Thyme:** Peak potency
- **Sage:** Excellent dried
- **Basil:** Dry before frost kills it
- **Peppermint:** Last vibrant leaves

**Drying process:**
1. Cut stems (morning after dew dries)
2. Bundle with twine (6–10 stems per bundle)
3. Hang upside down in cool, dark, airy place
4. 2–3 weeks drying typically
5. Leaves should crumble when dry
6. Store in sealed glass jars, cool dark place

### Making Tinctures

**Perfect time to start long-maceration tinctures:**
- Tincture herbs now
- 4–6 week soak
- Ready by November
- Perfect for winter immune season

**See [[Tincture Guide]] for full process**

### Making Syrups & Preparations

**Harvest heavily for:**
- Syrups (concentrated remedies)
- Salves (dried herb infusions)
- Herbal blends for winter

## 🌳 Summer Plants Declining

### Annuals Winding Down
- [[Basil]]: Still productive but frost warning coming
  - Harvest heavily now
  - Don't allow to set seed (unless want volunteers)
  - Dry for winter use
  - Make pesto for freezing

- [[Dill]]: Earlier sowings flowering, going to seed
  - Allow some to go to seed (volunteers next year funny
  - Harvest green dill for fresh use
  - Harvest seed clusters for drying

- [[Anise]]: Finishing for season
  - Let complete seed cycle
  - Harvest seed for teas, storage
  - Plant won't overwinter

### Tender Perennials Under Stress
- [[Peppermint]], [[Lemon Mint]]: Still productive but slowing
- Continue harvesting but noting reduced vigor

## 🌿 Harvest Notes

### Peak Harvest for Preservation
**Now is premium time for:**
- Oregano (peak essential oils)
- Marjoram (strongest flavor)
- Sage (concentrated compounds)
- Basil (before frost kills it)

**Timing:**
- Harvest in morning (after dew dries, before heat)
- When plants in flower (peak potency)
- Top ⅓ of growth

### What to Preserve
- Dried herbs (1–2 year storage)
- Tinctures (5–10 year storage)
- Syrups (refrigerated, 3–4 months)
- Oils (infused, 6–12 months)

---

## 🚰 Watering Changes

### Reduce Frequency
- Cooling season = less evaporation
- Rainfall typically increases (September can surprise)
- Check soil before watering
- Top inch dry = water time

### Focus on New Sowings
- Fall-planted cilantro + dill need consistent moisture until germinated
- Established perennials need less
